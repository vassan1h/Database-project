Linked Databases

Our database integrates data from leading biochemical databases to support gap-filling, metabolic modeling, and annotation for genome-scale metabolic models. Below, we describe the roles of KEGG, BiGG, and MetaCyc in our project.

KEGG

The Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) provides comprehensive data on metabolic pathways, reactions, and metabolites. In our project, KEGG identifiers are used during gap-filling to add missing reactions and metabolites to draft models, ensuring biochemical accuracy.

Visit KEGG

BiGG

The Biochemical, Genetic, and Genomic (BiGG) database offers curated metabolic models and reaction data. We use BiGG as a source for model templates and to cross-reference reactions during gap-filling, enhancing model completeness.

Visit BiGG

MetaCyc

MetaCyc is a database of metabolic pathways and enzymes, used alongside ModelSEED for annotation cross-referencing. It supports our database by providing detailed enzyme and pathway data to validate and refine model annotations.

Visit MetaCyc
